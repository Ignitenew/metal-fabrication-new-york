Steel Masters NYC — Brooklyn's Steel Fabricator Since 1972

Steel Masters NYC has been Brooklyn's trusted steel fabricator for more than five decades, building a reputation for precision craftsmanship, reliable permit management, and durable installations that stand up to New York City's demanding structural and environmental conditions. Founded in 1972 and based at 135 Liberty Ave in the heart of Brooklyn's 11212 ZIP code, the company has grown from a neighborhood ironworks shop into a full-service metal fabrication operation serving residential property owners, commercial building managers, contractors, and institutional clients across Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx. Every project — from a single sidewalk cellar door to a multi-floor structural steel alteration — is handled in-house from first measurement to final inspection.

The company's longevity in Brooklyn's competitive steel fabrication market is rooted in a straightforward operating philosophy: do the work correctly the first time, manage the permit process completely, and stand behind every installation. Steel Masters NYC's team of fabricators, welders, and installers has accumulated decades of combined experience on Brooklyn's most demanding project types, including custom steel staircases for pre-war brownstones, Economaster trash bin enclosures for commercial properties, expanded metal security cages, roof railings, rolldown gates, and structural steel alterations requiring NYC DOB engineering approval. The company's in-house fabrication shop ensures that quality is controlled at every stage, from raw steel through finished installation.

Brooklyn's built environment is among the most structurally diverse in the United States, with building stock ranging from 1880s masonry to modern steel-frame construction across ZIP codes that include 11212, 11207, 11221, and beyond. Steel Masters NYC's experience across this full range of building types is one of the company's most significant competitive advantages. The team brings site-specific knowledge to every project — understanding how Brooklyn's freeze-thaw cycles affect steel connections, how coastal salt air accelerates corrosion, and how DOB Brooklyn district office processing timelines shape project scheduling. This depth of local knowledge is something that cannot be replicated by contractors based outside the borough.
